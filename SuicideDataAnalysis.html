Analysis of Suicide Data in USA

Suicide Statistics

This study is an overview of Suicide trends and patterns in the United States (US). The annual suicide rate is 13.26 per 100,000 individuals.On average, there are 121 suicides per day. These graphs can be of use to public health officials, researchers, practitioners and the public to better understand the reasons of Suicide, population subgroups by Age, Sex, and Race at risk, and various Injury mechanisms used. With this analysis we should be able to provide insight for effective Suicide prevention efforts. This study also compares Year wise growth of these Rates.

Objective

The objective of this study is to identify :

  • Key affecting factors for Suicides
  • Identify the relationships between Suicide rates and Sex, Age category, Race, and Injury Mechanism of the person commiting Suicide
  • Create linear model using influencing predicates for Suicide rate
  • Evaluating model
  • Predicting model accuracy by using test data

Identifying key predictors influencing the suicide rate can help reduce suicides. Using this project we can understand various factors that should be prioritized for deeper study and policy for Suicide prevention.

Data Set

The data set has been taken from American Foundation for Suicide Prevention. In its raw form it has 87,360 rows and 16 columns. It gives information about mortality statistics in USA. It has data from 1999 to 2014. In this study, we concentrate on the analysis of solely Suicides as the Injury Intent. The data provides information about the Year in which fatality took place as well as the Sex, Age category, Race, and Injury Mechanism of the person profile committing suicide (how many people in that categorical combination in that year had a fatality, out of the sample population). As information is given regarding number of deaths and population, we add the derived variable SuicidesPer100k to scale suicide rate such that are results are not decimals and easier to understand. Important attributes can be defined as below :

  • Year : Year of Fatality
  • Sex : Gender of person committing Suicide
  • Age : Age of person committing Suicide
  • Race : Race of person committing Suicide
  • InjuryMechanism : Method used by person committing suicide.
  • InjuryIntent : Intention of fatality (we filter to just Suicides, so this column is eliminated).
  • Deaths : Number of deaths within the population.
  • Population : Total Population of the sample.
  • SuicidesPer100k : (Deaths * 100,000) / Population

Suicide Rates by Year

Next, lets analyze correlation between Suicide rate and Year

suicide.data.yearwise = suicide.data %>% group_by(Year) %>% summarise(totalSuicides = mean(SuicidesPer100k)) %>% 
    arrange(desc(totalSuicides))

ggplot(suicide.data.yearwise, aes(x = as.factor(suicide.data.yearwise$Year), y = suicide.data.yearwise$totalSuicides, 
    group = 1)) + geom_line(col = "steelblue", stat = "identity", size = 2) + labs(title = "Suicide Rates as per Year", 
    subtitle = "From Suicide statistics", y = "Suicide Rate", x = "Year") + scale_x_discrete(breaks = seq(1999, 
    2014, 5))

It can be seen that the Suicide rate decreased moving from 1999 to 2006, had a increase around 2007, later became static and then increased moving towards year 2014. Highest Suicide rate was observed in 2014. So the Year factor has a high correlation with the Suicide Ratio.

Lets observe the impact of Age on the average suicide rate per year .

avgsuiciderate.peragegroup.peryear = suicide.data %>% group_by_(.dots = c("Year",

ggplot(avgsuiciderate.peragegroup.peryear, aes(x = Year, y = totalSuicides)) + geom_line(aes(col = Age),

In 2014, the highest suicide rate (140) was among adults 75+ years of age. The second highest rate (130) occurred was among adults between 45 and 64 years of age. Younger groups have had consistently lower suicide rates than middle-aged and older adults. In 2014, adolescents and young adults aged 15 to 24 had maximum suicide rate of 90.

Suicide Rates by Injury Mechanism

Next, we analyse the correlation between Suicide rate and Injury Mechanism. Various injury mechanisms are used to commit suicide including Cut/pierce, Drowning, Fall, Fire/hot, Firearm etc. We analyze which injury mechanism is used most in committing Suicide.

suicide.injurymech.count = suicide.data %>% group_by(InjuryMechanism) %>% summarize(numberOfDeaths = sum(Deaths))
injurymech.barplot <- ggplot(suicide.injurymech.count, aes(x = "", y = numberOfDeaths, 
    fill = InjuryMechanism)) + geom_bar(width = 1, stat = "identity")
pie.chart <- injurymech.barplot + coord_polar(theta = "y") + theme_void()
pie.chart

It can be observed that firearms were the most common method of death by suicide, accounting for a little more than half of all suicide deaths. The next most common methods were suffocation (including hangings) and poisoning at 15.4%. Rest other injury mechanisms are very less in proportion to them.

In Conclusion:

  • Men die by suicide 3.5x more often than women
  • Firearms account for almost 50% of all suicides.
  • Most common suicidal injury mechanism used by men are Firearms while for women it is Poisoning.
  • Non Hispanic White males accounted for greater suicides
  • Highest total number of Suicides were done by people in the middle age group 45-64.
  • For period between 1999-2014 , Suicide rate is highest in old people 75+ of age and second highest in middle age people between 45 and 64 years of age

Based on the data available, it would be in the interest of public health to investigate further the warning signs of suicide among older men with access to firearms. Many individuals at 75+ do not live independently, so perhaps educating their caretakers (nursing homes, their adult children) of warning signs and how to be supportive would help.

One particular pitfall for this data is that it only records fatalities, not attempts. Just because a suicide attempt does not result in a fatality does not mean it is not serious and should not be investigated. That data would be invaluable in continuing this study.
